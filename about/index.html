How We Work.

Traditional agencies are segmented and siloed, leading to more time spent in project communication meetings than coming up with great, strategic creative. When you break down these barriers, creativity and strategy happen faster – leading to better results and happier clients.

We bring clients in sooner. We share seedlings of ideas to get to crazy big ideas faster. The front-end cycle of presentations and revisions to eventually getting the green light is compressed tremendously. Great stuff gets out faster, which means there’s more time to get out even more great stuff.

We call it “ideas in execution.” Because while it’s one thing to just have ideas, it’s another to execute great ones quickly and have enough time and budget left over to adjust and refine in real time.

1. Dig This is the exploratory phase where we work with you to fully understand your business and your challenges. We dive into the competitive landscape and search for the sweet spot where you can stand out and be different. It’s the seed that will grow into a positioning you can own.

2. Illuminate Here we use what we uncovered in the Dig. Many of the founding brand elements and strategies are formed in this phase. Based on findings, we craft insights that guide the marketing and media plans, target messaging, campaign development, and creative execution.

3. Crank This is where the gears turn faster and ideas are set in motion. Fueled by creativity and an illuminating strategy, we develop campaigns around the identified insights and work with our partners to build CRM and analytics tools, and marketing and media plan fulfillment tactics.

4. Execute In this phase we roll out the creative work, gather feedback and data, and celebrate cautiously.

5. Optimize What if we did this? What if we changed that? How could this be better? How could that work harder? In the final phase we ask a lot of questions, review results and tinker as needed to maximize impact and sales. Then there’s some back-patting and high-fiving. And then some more question asking and tinkering.
